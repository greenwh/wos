# WOS Buff Strategy Roadmap

## Complete Guide to March & Combat Effectiveness

**Scope:** Every system that contributes to troop stats (Attack, Defense, Lethality, Health), deployment capacity, march speed, and rally effectiveness on a Gen 1/2 server.

**Updated:** March 2026

---

## The Buff Ecosystem at a Glance

Your total combat power comes from layering **14 distinct buff systems** on top of base troop stats. The systems interact multiplicatively with each other in many cases — meaning a 10% bump from one system is worth more when your base stats from other systems are already high. This is why spreading investment across all systems matters more than maxing any single one.

### Stat Priority for Combat

Based on the damage formula and community testing:

1. **Lethality** — bypasses defense, directly damages HP. #1 offensive stat for rallies.
2. **Attack** — breaks enemy defense. Core damage component. #2 offensive stat.
3. **Defense** — absorbs incoming attack damage. Key for tanks and garrison.
4. **Health** — total HP pool. Determines how long troops survive after defense breaks.
5. **Deployment Capacity** — more troops = more damage and survivability.
6. **March Speed** — tactical advantage for repositioning, hitting first.

## System 1: Hero Gear (Unlocks at Furnace 15)

### What It Does
Each hero equips 4 gear pieces (Goggles, Gloves, Belt, Boots) that provide hero stats (Attack, Defense, Health) and troop command stats (Troop Lethality and Troop Health).

### Stat Mapping by Slot
- **Goggles + Boots** → Hero Attack + Troop **Lethality** (offensive pieces)
- **Gloves + Belt** → Hero Health + Troop **Health** (defensive pieces)

### Priority by Troop Type
- Infantry heroes: **Gloves + Belt** first (tanking role = Health priority)
- Lancer heroes: **Goggles + Boots** first (balanced but lean offensive)
- Marksman heroes: **Goggles + Boots** first (pure damage role = Lethality priority)

### Rarity Tiers
Empty → Common (Gray) → Rare (Blue) → Epic (Purple) → Mythic (Orange)

Each rarity tier is a massive stat jump. A Mythic piece at +30 dramatically outperforms an Epic piece at +50.

### Enhancement & Master Forgery
- Enhancement: +1 through +60+, each level adds stats
- Master Forgery (MF): Levels 1–5, available on Mythic gear, each MF level is a significant stat bump
- **Focus MF on one piece at a time** — MF3 on one piece beats MF1 on three pieces

### Checklist Items
- [ ] Mythic priority pieces for each active hero (by troop type)
- [ ] Priority pieces enhanced to +45 minimum
- [ ] MF3+ on priority pieces before touching secondary pieces
- [ ] Secondary pieces (non-priority slots) at Mythic, enhanced to +30+

## System 2: Hero Exclusive Gear / Widgets (Unlocks at 1★ Ascension)

### What It Does
Gold-quality (Mythic) heroes unlock a 5th gear slot — their exclusive weapon. It provides Hero Attack/Defense/Health AND Troop Health/Lethality command buffs. Additionally, it grants 2 exclusive skills.

### Widget Scaling
Widgets are the upgrade material. Total cost to max: 275 widgets per hero.

| Level | Troop Buff % |
|-------|-------------|
| 2 | 5% |
| 4 | 7.5% |
| 6 | 10% |
| 8 | 12.5% |
| 10 (Max) | 15% |

### Special Bonus Mechanic
Widget buffs are **Special Bonuses** — they apply multiplicatively on top of base combat stats. The formula is: `(Base Stats × Special Bonus %) + Special Bonus %`. This means they scale harder as your base stats grow.

### Which Heroes' Widgets Matter Most
For rallies: The rally leader's widget applies, plus the top 4 rally joiners contribute their lead hero's 1st Expedition Skill. Widget bonuses come from the rally leader's hero specifically.

### Checklist Items
- [ ] Flint widget: target level 6+ (10% buff threshold)
- [ ] Molly widget: target level 6+
- [ ] Alonzo widget: Captain Ahab — 15% rally lethality at max (long-term)
- [ ] Widget sources: Enigma events, Mystery Shop, packs

## System 3: Hero Skills

### What It Does
Exploration skills drive Arena and hero-vs-hero combat. Expedition skills drive all troop-based combat (rallies, garrison, AC, Foundry Battle, Labyrinth).

### Skill Level Impact
Each level (1–5) provides a meaningful percentage bump. The Lv3→4 and Lv4→5 jumps are especially large for key skills like Burning Resolve, Stand at Arms, and Snow's Grace.

### Rally Joiner Mechanic
When joining rallies, only the **1st Expedition skill** (top-right skill) of your lead hero contributes to the rally's buff pool. The top 4 joiners' skills apply. Higher-level skills replace lower-level ones from earlier joiners.

### Checklist Items
- [ ] All active roster heroes: Exploration skills at Lv4 (Arena)
- [ ] All active roster heroes: Expedition skills at Lv4 (rallies/PvP)
- [ ] Key Lv5 targets identified (Burning Resolve, Stand at Arms, Snow's Grace)
- [ ] Rally joiner hero selection optimized for 1st Expedition Skill

## System 4: Pets (Unlocks at Furnace 18 / Server Age 55+ days)

### What They Do
Pets provide two layers of buffs:
1. **Passive Stats** — from leveling and refinement, always active
2. **Active Skills** — manually activated, 2-hour duration, ~20-hour cooldown

### Passive Stat Sources
- **Pet Leveling** → Troop Attack and Defense bonuses
- **Pet Refinement** → Troop Lethality and Health bonuses

Refinement uses Wild Marks (Common/Advanced) and is RNG-based. Target purple-tier stats minimum.

### Combat Active Skills (2-hour buffs)

| Pet | Skill | Effect | Priority |
|-----|-------|--------|----------|
| **Cave Lion** | Feral Anthem | +10% All Troop Attack | S-Tier |
| **Saber-Tooth Tiger** | Apex Assault | +10% Troop Lethality | S-Tier |
| **Frost Gorilla** | Earthbound Vigor | +10% Troop Health | S-Tier |
| **Mammoth** | Hardened Skin | +10% Troop Defense | A-Tier |
| **Snow Ape** | Tumbling Power | +15,000 Squad Capacity | A-Tier |
| **Iron Rhino** | Rallying Beast | +150,000 Rally Capacity | A-Tier (Rally Leaders) |
| **Titan Roc** | Armor Rift | -5% Enemy Health | A-Tier |
| **Snow Leopard** | Lightning Raid | +30% March Speed, -5% Enemy Lethality | B-Tier (situational) |

### Pre-Battle Activation
Before any competitive event (Foundry, AC, Bear Hunt, SvS), activate relevant combat pet skills. They last 2 hours — time them for the event window. Pet skills also auto-activate in Labyrinth (Cave of Monsters and Gaia Heart zones).

### Checklist Items
- [ ] All combat pets unlocked and leveling
- [ ] Refinement: focus on Cave Lion, Saber-Tooth Tiger, Frost Gorilla first
- [ ] Pre-event routine: activate Cave Lion + Saber-Tooth (offense) or Mammoth + Frost Gorilla (defense)
- [ ] Snow Ape activated for rally events (extra deployment)
- [ ] Iron Rhino for rally leaders specifically

## System 5: Chief Gear (Unlocks at Furnace 22)

### What It Does
Six equipment pieces for your Chief that provide **Troop Attack and Defense** bonuses per troop type.

### Gear Pieces by Troop Type
- **Coat + Pants** → Infantry Attack & Defense
- **Ring + Weapon (Cane)** → Marksman Attack & Defense
- **Cap (Helmet) + Watch** → Lancer Attack & Defense

### Set Bonuses (Critical)
- **3 pieces of same quality** → All Troop Defense bonus
- **6 pieces of same quality** → All Troop Attack bonus

The set bonuses are why you craft all 6 pieces before enhancing, and keep them within 1–2 quality levels of each other.

### Quality Tiers
Uncommon → Rare → Epic → Legendary/Mythic

### Enhancement
Requires Hardened Alloy + Polishing Solution. Sources: Polar Terror rallies (Lv3+), Beast Hunting (Lv22+), Crazy Joe, Foundry Shop, AC Shop.

### Upgrade Order
1. Infantry (Coat/Pants) — frontline survivability
2. Marksman (Ring/Cane) — damage output
3. Lancer (Helmet/Watch) — balanced
4. Keep all 6 within 1–2 levels for set bonuses

### Checklist Items
- [ ] All 6 chief gear pieces crafted (even low quality)
- [ ] 3-piece set bonus active (Defense)
- [ ] 6-piece set bonus active (Attack)
- [ ] Enhancement priority: Infantry → Marksman → Lancer
- [ ] Pieces kept within 1–2 quality levels of each other

## System 6: Chief Charms (Unlocks at Furnace 25–26)

### What They Do
18 charm slots (3 per chief gear piece) that boost **Troop Lethality and Health** — the stats that Chief Gear doesn't cover. Together, Chief Gear + Charms cover all 4 combat stats.

### Charm Organization by Troop Type
- Helmet + Armor charms → Infantry Lethality & Health
- Weapon + Belt charms → Marksman Lethality & Health
- Watch + Boots charms → Lancer Lethality & Health

### Charm Levels
Levels 1–16. Starting at Level 4, each major level has 4 sub-levels (4.0 → 4.1 → 4.2 → 4.3 → 5). Materials: Charm Guides (basic), Charm Designs (intermediate), Jewel Secrets (advanced, Lv12+).

### Uniformity Bonus
Keeping all charms at roughly the same level activates a uniformity bonus — additional Attack and Defense for all troops. Don't over-level one charm at the expense of others.

### Checklist Items
- [ ] All 18 charm slots populated
- [ ] Charms leveled evenly (uniformity bonus active)
- [ ] Priority: Infantry charms → Marksman → Lancer (mirrors Chief Gear order)
- [ ] Save charm upgrades for SvS/KoI/Officer Project events (double-dip on event points)

## System 7: Research Center — Battle Tree

### What It Does
Permanent percentage buffs to troop stats, deployment capacity, march capacity, and troop training.

### Key Battle Research (by impact)

| Research | Effect | Priority |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Regimental Expansion | +March/Rally Capacity | Very High |
| Infantry/Lancer/Marksman Attack | +% Troop Attack by type | High |
| Infantry/Lancer/Marksman Defense | +% Troop Defense by type | High |
| Infantry/Lancer/Marksman Lethality | +% Troop Lethality by type | High |
| Infantry/Lancer/Marksman Health | +% Troop Health by type | High |
| Command Tactics | +March Queues | High (early game) |
| Tool Enhancement | Research Speed | High (accelerates everything) |

### Research Order Philosophy
1. Tool Enhancement (speed up everything else)
2. Command Tactics (march queues)
3. Regimental Expansion (more troops per march)
4. Type-specific stats: Infantry Defense first (tanking), then spread across types

### Checklist Items
- [ ] Tool Enhancement maxed for current Research Center level
- [ ] Regimental Expansion at highest available level
- [ ] Battle tree stats leveled evenly across troop types
- [ ] Research speed buffs stacked before starting long projects

## System 8: War Academy / Fire Crystal Tech (Unlocks at Furnace 30)

### What It Does
Advanced technology tree requiring Fire Crystals. Unlocks T11 troops and provides powerful stat multipliers beyond what the Research Center offers. Also governs Fire Crystal troop upgrades with special skills.

### Impact
Earthlab Labyrinth zone uses Research Center + War Academy stats exclusively. This is a major long-term investment — expensive and slow to develop.

### Checklist Items
- [ ] War Academy built at Furnace 30
- [ ] Fire Crystal research prioritized: troop stats → deployment → speed
- [ ] T11 troop training unlocked and underway
- [ ] Fire Crystal troop skills leveled (passive combat buffs at certain FC levels)

## System 11: Gem Buffs (Temporary Purchases)

### What They Do
Time-limited combat buffs purchasable with gems or earned from events/shops. Cannot stack same type.

### Available Gem Buffs

| Buff | 2-Hour (cheaper) | 12-Hour (better value) |
|------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Troops Attack Up | +10% | +20% |
| Troops Defense Up | +10% | +20% |
| Troops Lethality Up | +10% | +20% |
| Troops Health Up | +10% | +20% |
| Enemy Attack Down | -10% | -20% |
| Enemy Defense Down | -10% | -20% |
| Deployment Capacity Up | +10% | +20% |

### When to Use
- **Always before major events:** Foundry Battle, AC, SvS Castle Battle, Bear Trap
- **AC registration trick:** Activate buffs, register your AC team, and the buffs persist on the registered team even after they expire
- Stack with pet skills and widget bonuses for maximum Special Bonus multiplication

### Cost Efficiency
12-hour buffs cost significantly more gems but are better for extended events (SvS, Foundry). 2-hour buffs are better for single rallies. Stockpile free buff items from events rather than buying with gems when possible.

### Checklist Items
- [ ] Buff items stockpiled from events/shops
- [ ] Pre-event activation routine established
- [ ] AC team registered with active buffs for best snapshot
- [ ] Gem budget allocated for event-day buffs

## System 12: Daybreak Island (Unlocks at Furnace 19)

### What It Does
Island decorations provide permanent stat buffs. The Tree of Life is the centerpiece — upgrading it provides global bonuses.

### Tree of Life Buffs
- Level 5: +Troops Attack
- Level 10: +Troops Lethality
- Various levels: Healing Speed, Troop Capacity, Defense

### Key Decorations (Combat-Relevant)

| Decoration | Max Level | Buff |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| Luminary Citadel | 10 | +10% Troops Attack (from Labyrinth rewards) |
| Hero's Sanctum | 5 | +2.5% Troops Defense (from Labyrinth rewards) |
| Basic Dock | 10 | +1,000 Deployment Capacity |
| Barbecue Stand | Max | +5% Training Speed |
| Starry Lighthouse | 10 | Multiple buffs (unlocked at Tree of Life Lv10) |
| Type-specific decorations | Varies | +Attack or +Defense for specific troop types |

### Prosperity System
Decorations generate Prosperity → Prosperity unlocks Tree of Life levels. Limited/event decorations give the most Prosperity. Prioritize buff-giving decorations that can be upgraded over cosmetic ones.

### Checklist Items
- [ ] Tree of Life: target Level 5 (Attack buff), then Level 10 (Lethality)
- [ ] Luminary Citadel copies collected and upgraded (Labyrinth rewards)
- [ ] Hero's Sanctum copies collected and upgraded
- [ ] Basic Dock upgraded for deployment capacity
- [ ] Prosperity maximized through smart decoration placement

## System 13: Skins

### What They Do
Cosmetic skins that provide stacking combat bonuses. The more unique skins you own, the higher the bonus.

### Skin Types and Buffs

| Skin Type | Buff Per Skin |
|-----------|--------------|
| **City Skins** | +2% Troop Attack each |
| **Teleport Skins** | +2% Troop Lethality each |
| **March Skins** | +5% March Speed each (Hunting only) |
| **Avatar Frames** | Cosmetic only (no combat buff) |

### Stacking
Bonuses stack — owning 3 city skins = +6% Troop Attack. This is a significant free damage boost.

### How to Get Skins
- Events (SvS rewards, seasonal events)
- Packs (some paid, some F2P milestone rewards)
- Codes (occasionally include temporary avatar frames)
- City skin chests and teleport skin chests from progression milestones

### Checklist Items
- [ ] All available free city skins collected
- [ ] All available free teleport skins collected
- [ ] Skin bonuses verified (Profile → check active skin buffs)
- [ ] Event skins prioritized when offered as rewards

## System 14: Alliance Territory & Facilities

### What They Do
Controlling alliance territory and facilities provides passive buffs to all alliance members within the territory.

### Key Facility Buffs
- **Expedition Facilities:** +15% March Speed at Lv3
- **Gathering Facilities:** +5% Gathering Speed
- **Various combat facilities:** +Troop Attack, Defense

### Territory Bonuses
Staying within your alliance's banner network provides Special Bonus stats. These are territorial — you lose them if you leave the territory.

### Checklist Items
- [ ] Alliance actively controls key combat facilities
- [ ] Cities positioned within alliance territory for passive buffs
- [ ] Facility upgrade contributions maintained

## System 15: Castle Battle Titles & Minister Positions

### What They Do
Castle Battle winners can appoint Ministers and a Supreme President. These positions grant powerful combat buffs to the holder and (in the President's case) state-wide buffs.

### Minister Buffs
Various minister roles provide individual combat buffs (Troop Attack, Defense, etc.) to the appointed player.

### Supreme President
Provides state-wide Lethality buff — benefits every player on the winning state.

### Where They Work
Most PvP modes EXCEPT Frostfire Mine. They DO work in general PvP, rallies, garrison, and AC.

### Checklist Items
- [ ] Participate in Castle Battle for title opportunities
- [ ] If appointed: buffs active and leveraged for competitive events
- [ ] State-wide President buff: coordinate timing with AC registration

## System 16: Troop Tier & Fire Crystal Troops

### What They Do
Higher-tier troops have exponentially better base stats. All percentage buffs from other systems multiply on top of these base stats — meaning the difference between T9 and T11 is massive after all multipliers apply.

### Troop Skills (Passive)
At certain troop levels, passive skills unlock:
- Infantry: +10% Attack vs Lancers (Lv1), +10% Defense vs Lancers (Lv7)
- Marksman: +10% Attack vs Infantry (similar pattern)
- Lancer: +10% Attack vs Marksmen
- Fire Crystal levels add additional passive % buffs

### Promotion Priority
Always promote lower-tier troops to highest available tier. Lower-tier troops waste Infirmary space and are net negatives in competitive combat.

### Checklist Items
- [ ] All troops promoted to highest available tier
- [ ] Lower-tier troops dismissed or promoted (not deployed)
- [ ] Fire Crystal troop research progressing (post-Furnace 30)
- [ ] T11 goal set (War Academy prerequisite)

## Where Buffs Work (Quick Reference)

Not all buffs apply everywhere. Key exceptions:

| Mode | What Works | What Doesn't Work |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **General PvP** | Everything | — |
| **Foundry Battle** | Everything | — |
| **AC** | Snapshot at registration | Buffs that expire after registration still apply to registered team |
| **Frostfire Mine** | Most | Castle Battle titles, Minister positions, march speed buffs, gathering buffs |
| **Canyon Clash** | Most | Deployment buffs, pet gem buffs, march speed |
| **Labyrinth (Gaia Heart)** | Almost all | Castle Battle positions, alliance tech/territory, gem buffs, facilities |
| **Labyrinth (other zones)** | Zone-specific only | Everything outside that zone's stat source |

*Every buff system is a brick in the wall. No single system wins battles alone — but the player who has invested across all 14 systems will outperform someone who maxed one and ignored the rest. Spread smart, then deepen.*
