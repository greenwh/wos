## Key Principle: Where Your Skills Actually Apply

This matters for everything that follows.

**Arena and Exploration stages → Exploration skills.** These are the hero-vs-hero battles where your heroes fight directly. Skill levels directly scale hero damage, defense, and survivability in these fights.

**Labyrinth, Garrison, Rally, AC, Foundry Battle → Expedition skills.** These govern troop-based combat where your heroes lead armies. Expedition skills buff troop stats (attack, defense, health, lethality) and are the backbone of competitive PvP.

Both matter. The roadmap prioritizes both skill trees but sequences them based on where you'll feel the impact fastest.

## Phase 1: Immediate Priorities (Next 2–4 Weeks)

### Gear: Finish Molly's Priority Pieces

Molly's Goggles (+45 MF1) and Boots (+23 MF2) are your best return on enhancement investment right now. The priority pieces for Lancers are Goggles and Boots — you already have them at Mythic, they just need leveling.

**Action items:**
- Push Molly's **Boots from +23 to +45** minimum. This is the biggest single-piece improvement available on your roster right now. The stat jump from +23 to +45 is enormous.
- Continue pushing Molly's **Goggles toward +52–55** range. They're already at +45 MF1 — solid, but more enhancement here directly improves Arena performance.
- Push Molly's **Goggles MF to MF2** when Essence Stones allow. Each MF level is a meaningful stat bump.

**Why Molly first?** Flint's priority pieces (Gloves/Belt) are already maxed at +60 MF3. Molly's priority pieces are Mythic but under-enhanced. Getting her closer to Flint's level gives you two strong frontline heroes instead of one.

### Gear: Push Bahiti's Priority Pieces Higher

Bahiti's Goggles (+52 MF2) and Boots (+43 MF3) are in good shape but not capped. As your primary Marksman until Alonzo is ready, every point matters.

**Action items:**
- Push Bahiti's **Goggles to +55–60** range.
- Push Bahiti's **Boots to +50** minimum. The MF3 on boots is great — just needs more raw enhancement.
- Push Bahiti's **Goggles MF to MF3** when possible.

### Skills: Bahiti's Exploration Skills to Lv4

Bahiti's Exploration skills are all stuck at Lv3 (Precise Shot, Quick Shot, Pathfinder Vision). In Arena, where Exploration skills drive performance, this puts Bahiti a full tier behind Flint and Molly who are all at Lv4.

**Action items:**
- Level **Precise Shot to Lv4** first — it's the primary damage skill.
- Then **Quick Shot to Lv4** — secondary damage.
- Then **Pathfinder Vision to Lv4** — utility/support.
- Cost: 3 skills × (Lv3→4) = moderate Skill Manual investment but very high Arena impact.

### Skills: Gina's Expedition Skills

Gina's Expedition skills are the weakest on your active roster: Endurance Training Lv3 and Quick Paced Lv2. If Gina is joining rallies or contributing to Foundry Battle, her Expedition skills need attention.

**Action items:**
- Level **Quick Paced to Lv3** — closing the gap from Lv2 to Lv3 is cheap and impactful.
- Level **Endurance Training to Lv4** when manuals allow.
- Note: Gina's long-term role is outbound reinforcement, not garrison. Don't over-invest — she's a placeholder until your roster evolves.

## Phase 3: Alonzo Transition (Weeks 8–16)

### Building Alonzo as Bahiti's Replacement

Alonzo (Mythic Marksman, currently 1★ 1/6, all skills Lv1–2) is the long-term plan. He's a generational upgrade over Bahiti — his AoE damage, crowd control (Trapnet), and exclusive gear (Captain Ahab with 15% rally lethality buff) make him a top-tier Marksman across multiple game modes.

**The transition plan:**

**Step 1 — Accumulate shards.** Alonzo is available through Hall of Heroes and Daily Deals. Target **3★ minimum** before activating him (preferably 4★ for F2P value, achievable across ~3 Lucky Wheel cycles per your established strategy). Don't rush him onto the active roster at 1★ — Bahiti at 4★ with developed gear outperforms a low-star Alonzo.

**Step 2 — Skill investment (start early).** Even before Alonzo is roster-ready, begin leveling his key skills as manuals allow:

*Exploration (Arena priority):*
- **Trapnet to Lv3** first — his signature AoE + immobilize. This is what makes him an Arena monster.
- **Tidal Force to Lv3** — secondary AoE damage.
- **Harpoon Blast to Lv3** — passive stun utility.

*Expedition (Rally/Labyrinth priority):*
- **Onslaught to Lv3** first — primary troop buff.
- **Iron Strength to Lv3** — defensive troop buff.
- **Poison Harpoon to Lv3** — damage over time.

**Step 3 — Gear transfer.** When Alonzo is ready to replace Bahiti, transfer Bahiti's Mythic Goggles and Boots directly to Alonzo. Same troop type = free transfer, no resource cost. This is why you're investing in Bahiti's priority pieces now — they become Alonzo's foundation.

**Step 4 — Weapon leveling.** Alonzo already has Captain Ahab (Legendary). Prioritize upgrading it when widgets become available — the exclusive skills (Ocean's Bounty healing + Harpoon Enhancement rally lethality) are game-changers for both Arena and Expedition modes.

## Phase 4: Lv5 Skill Pushes and Endgame Gear (Ongoing)

### Skill Lv5 Targets (Requires 4★+ Heroes)

Once heroes reach 4★, you can push key skills to Lv5. This is expensive in Skill Manuals, so be selective. Target the skills with the biggest percentage jumps:

**Flint (once at 4★):**
- **Burning Resolve Lv5** (Expedition) — the 25% troop attack buff at max level makes him an elite rally leader. This is the #1 Lv5 target on your entire roster.
- **Fires of Vengeance Lv5** (Exploration) — primary Arena damage skill.

**Molly (once at 4★, almost there):**
- **Snow's Grace Lv5** (Expedition) — primary troop buff for Lancer combat.
- **Super Snowball Lv5** (Exploration) — primary Arena damage.

**Jessie:**
- **Stand at Arms Lv5** (Expedition) — the 25% all-troop damage buff at max level. Jessie's entire value is this one skill. Already at Lv4, so the Lv5 push is the ultimate target.

**Bahiti/Alonzo:**
- Whoever is your active Marksman when manuals are available. For Bahiti, **Sixth Sense Lv5** (Expedition). For Alonzo, **Trapnet Lv5** (Exploration) once he's built up.

### Endgame Gear Targets

**Non-priority Mythic pieces:** Once your priority pieces are all at +50–60 MF3+, begin acquiring Mythic Goggles/Boots for Flint and Mythic Gloves/Belt for Molly. These are secondary stat slots but at Mythic rarity they still provide meaningful gains. Enhancement target: +30–40 range before diminishing returns bite hard.

**Chief Gear (Furnace 22):** When you unlock chief gear, the priority order is Infantry (Jacket/Pants) → Marksman (Ring/Cane) → Lancer (Helmet/Watch). Craft all six pieces first before enhancing, then level them evenly in sets of 3 to activate set bonuses (Defense at 3-piece, Attack at 6-piece). Keep all pieces within 1–2 levels of each other.

## What NOT to Spend Resources On

- **Sergey's gear beyond current levels.** His Epic +50 Gloves/Belt are fine for his defensive garrison role. Don't Mythic anything for Sergey — those resources go to Flint.
- **Gina's Exploration skills.** Her Exploration kit (Eagle Eyes, Incendiary Arrow, Windtalker) is decent for beast hunting content but she's a secondary hero. Don't push past Lv4.
- **Bench hero skills.** Natalia, Zinman, Jasser, Patrick, and others on the bench should stay as-is unless their role changes. Every manual spent on a bench hero is a manual not spent on your top 6.
- **Reforging well-enhanced pieces.** Your Epic +50/+59/+60 pieces on Gina, Sergey, and Jessie still provide value. Reforging at these levels to fund other heroes is inefficient — the recovery rate doesn't justify it.
- **Spreading MF levels thin.** Focus MF investment on one piece at a time. Getting a single piece to MF3 beats getting three pieces to MF1.
